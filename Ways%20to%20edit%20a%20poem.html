Ways to edit a poem

18th March 2021 at 4:00pm
blog editing poetry

Here are some ideas and techniques if you are stuck on rewriting a poem.

Remember that the whole point of editing is to Edit through someone else's eyes. You need to not be in love with the poem.

  • Read your poem out loud to hear the sound of it.
  • Where does the poem surprise you? Make these accidents intentional. Build up to them or go in different directions.
  • What is the imagery doing? Is it coherent? Is it mostly Concrete or abstract? Are you showing or telling?
  • Does the form reflect the content? Is there a more appropriate form that would work? Can you build in rhyme or is the rhyme inapropriate?

Transform the poem and create something new out of the ashes.

  • Use a line as a new poem
  • Create a Negative of a poem
  • Combine three images from seperate poems or seperate sources.
  • Condense to a single sentence
  • Cut upthe poem.
  • Look at line breaks. What are they doing? Are they intentional?
  • Remove clichés
  • Remove joints of poem and common words. Look for vague, abstract concepts like 'love' 'beauty' etc. Show don't tell!
  • Keep a slush pile
